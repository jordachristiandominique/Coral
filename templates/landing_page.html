How It Works

From underwater field capture to decision-ready insights, CoralSense follows a clear end-to-end workflow designed for research teams in the Davao Gulf.

Field Survey

Researchers capture underwater photos during scheduled reef monitoring dives.

Step 01

Upload & Tag

Images are uploaded with GPS coordinates to preserve exact survey locations.

Step 02

Automatic Analysis

OpenCV processes each image set and computes coral coverage metrics automatically.

Step 03

Real-Time Results

Biodiversity class is assigned instantly to surface reef condition as soon as analysis finishes.

Step 04

Dashboard Insights

About the Project

Research Collaboration for Reef Protection

CoralSense is an academic and field research initiative built to support marine ecosystem monitoring and evidence-based conservation planning in the Davao Gulf.

SDG Alignment

SDG 14: Life Below Water

This platform aligns with United Nations SDG 14 by helping teams measure reef condition, biodiversity trends, and restoration priorities.

SDG 14 Aligned

Partnership

DNSC Research Partnership

Developed in collaboration with DNSC to strengthen marine science workflows, improve field data reliability, and support student-led and faculty-led coastal research.

Focus Area

Davao Gulf

Reef Status Guide

Biodiversity Classification

Class A - High coral coverage

Coral Coverage: ≥ 60%

Reef with abundant live coral cover and high biodiversity.

Class B - Moderate coral coverage

Coral Coverage: 40-59%

Reef with moderate live coral cover and stable recovery potential.

Class C - Low coral coverage

Coral Coverage: < 40%

Reef with low live coral cover, requiring monitoring and conservation efforts.
